Leadership trait is one of the most sought after trait of personality in modern
world. There is a huge debate on what qualities make a person a good leader.
Some people believe that for an leader to be most effective he or she should
have a sound moral judgement rather than ability to maintain the respect of his
or her peers. This essay will discuss the argument in detail and put forward two
reasons to support why a great moral judgement is more important than ability
to maintain the respect of his or her peers.

Leaders have the ability to change the course of mankind. History has been a
huge evidence all over the world for this case. It is absolutely necessary
for human race to adapt through the time to its circumstances and be
progressive. For that purpose a leader having well calibrated moral
compass essential. A person with charge with poor moral judgment while
seeking glory and progress often overlooks human rights,equality and justice.
One of the core reasons behind catastrophic events such as World War I and
World War II was people in power with unchecked poor moral compass.
It clearly indicates that person without good morals ultimatley brings chaos
disturbing balance of the society

Further, respect is a powerful human emotion that is a factor in
leader-follower relation. Hence while choosing a leader it should be consider
if the respect is mutual. Non conditional respect to leader feeds the ego.
A leader who is well respected by his peers and shares the same feeling with
them is in modern terms of leadership is a brilliant leader. Successful sports
teams like FC Barcelona or Indian cricket team share a common factor of mutual
respect between leadership and other memebrs. Uni-directional respect to the
leaders forms a divide between leadership and other people.

One might argue that a leader who rules with utmost respect by his peer
can work more efficiently and freely. But isnt that the reason why we should
have a leader with moral judgement and mutual respect? There must be some
accountibility and responsibility to the leader and his decision and it only
comes with sound moral judgement and mutual feeling of respect. Preferring
moral compass in leadership quality will be a positive lesson learned from
history.
